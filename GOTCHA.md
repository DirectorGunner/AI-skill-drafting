# Skill Drafting — Gotchas

- Do not make a skill a general advice essay. Keep it tied to one repeatable job with clear triggers and outputs.
- Do not bury trigger rules only in the body. The frontmatter `description` is the main trigger surface.
- Do not put long source digests in `SKILL.md`. Put detailed material in references and route to it.
- Do not claim quality based on self-assessment alone. Define an external or observable check before implementation.
- Do not let subagents write overlapping shipped files. Use them for independent review, source digestion, or forward-testing; keep final integration with the orchestrator.
- Do not edit `AGENTS.md`, `CLAUDE.md`, settings, hooks, or permissions just because a skill would benefit from them. Propose the change and ask.
